# Johannes January - 6-Month Career Development Plan

Period: June 2026 to December 2026

Primary career targets:
- Business Analyst
- Data Analyst
- Business Intelligence Analyst
- Sales Analytics Analyst
- Operations Analytics Analyst
- Project Analyst / Project Coordinator roles with analytics responsibilities

Positioning:
Johannes January is an analytics-trained business professional and M.S. Applied AI & Business Analytics candidate at Quinnipiac University with 8+ years of experience across retail, manufacturing, sales operations, customer service, merchandising, and business analytics. This plan is designed to strengthen his U.S. job-market readiness through certifications, portfolio evidence, and independent research.

## Goal 1: Certification Roadmap

Objective:
Build a balanced certification profile that supports analytics, business intelligence, project management, cloud literacy, and AI fluency.

Strategy:
- Complete 3 major certifications over 6 months, using roughly 2 months per certification.
- Complete 6 lower-cost certificates or micro-credentials, one per month, to build momentum and add visible evidence to LinkedIn, Handshake, the resume, and the portfolio.
- Treat StackCert-style pages as inspiration for structure: outcome, career fit, skills covered, timeline, weekly study plan, proof of completion, and job-market positioning.

### Major Certification 1: Microsoft Certified: Power BI Data Analyst Associate (PL-300)

Timing:
June - July 2026

Career fit:
This should be the first major certification because Power BI appears frequently in U.S. analyst, BI, sales analytics, operations analytics, and reporting roles. It directly supports dashboard development, KPI reporting, data modeling, Power Query, DAX, stakeholder reporting, and business decision support.

Best-fit target roles:
- Business Intelligence Analyst
- Data Analyst
- Sales Analytics Analyst
- Operations Analyst
- Reporting Analyst

Core skills to master:
- Power BI data preparation
- Power Query
- Data modeling
- DAX measures
- Dashboard design
- Report publishing and sharing
- Business requirements translation
- KPI storytelling

Portfolio output required:
Build one polished Power BI dashboard project using a sales, operations, or customer-service dataset. The project should include a business problem, stakeholder requirements, cleaned data model, dashboard screenshots, KPI definitions, and written recommendations.

Weekly plan:
- Week 1: Review exam guide, identify weak areas, refresh Power BI basics.
- Week 2: Power Query, data cleaning, relationships, star schema.
- Week 3: DAX measures, calculated columns, time intelligence.
- Week 4: Dashboard design, slicers, drill-through, KPI cards.
- Week 5: Practice assessments and weak-area review.
- Week 6: Build portfolio project.
- Week 7: Full practice exam and corrections.
- Week 8: Sit for exam or final readiness review.

Resume wording after completion:
Microsoft Certified: Power BI Data Analyst Associate (PL-300)

## Goal 2: Industry Portfolio Roadmap

Objective:
Build an extensive project portfolio tailored to Business Analyst, Data Analyst, BI Analyst, Sales Analytics, and Operations Analytics roles using tools gained through Quinnipiac coursework and self-study.

Portfolio strategy:
The portfolio should not look like a student assignment archive. It should look like a business evidence library. Each project should answer a practical business question, use a real or realistic dataset, show tools used, and end with recommendations.

Recommended portfolio sections:
- Business Analytics Projects
- BI Dashboards
- Sales and Operations Analytics
- AI and Predictive Analytics
- SQL and Data Cleaning
- Business Analysis Artifacts
- Research and Thought Leadership

Target roles:
Sales Analytics Analyst, BI Analyst, Data Analyst

Business question:
Which products, customer segments, and regions drive revenue growth, and where are margin or sales-efficiency risks emerging?

Tools:
Power BI, Excel, SQL, DAX

Deliverables:
- Executive dashboard
- Sales trend analysis
- Customer or product segmentation
- KPI dictionary
- SQL cleaning script
- 1-page business recommendation memo

### Project 2: Retail Operations KPI Dashboard

Target roles:
Operations Analyst, BI Analyst, Business Analyst

Tools:
Tableau, Excel, SQL

Deliverables:
- Branch performance dashboard
- KPI benchmarking view
- Store expansion or merchandising scorecard
- Written recommendations

### Project 3: Customer Service and Back-Order Analytics

Target roles:
Operations Analytics Analyst, Business Analyst, Data Analyst

What patterns drive back-order delays, customer complaints, and service bottlenecks?

Tools:
Python, SQL, Excel, Power BI

Deliverables:
- Data-cleaning notebook
- Delay and issue categorization

- Dashboard or visual summary
- Process-improvement recommendation memo

### Project 4: Predictive Sales Forecasting

Target roles:
Data Analyst, Sales Analytics Analyst, Operations Analyst

Business question:
Can historical sales patterns be used to forecast demand and support inventory or sales planning?

Tools:
Python, pandas, scikit-learn or statsmodels, Excel

Deliverables:

- Forecast visualization

Tools:

Deliverables:

Business Analyst, Project Analyst, Operations Analyst

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

Recommended weekly commitment:
- Certification study: 5-7 hours per week
- Portfolio build: 4-6 hours per week
- Research and writing: 2-3 hours per week
- Job-market/application preparation: 1-2 hours per week

Weekly structure:
- Monday: Certification lesson or exam topic
- Tuesday: Hands-on tool practice
- Wednesday: Portfolio build
- Thursday: Certification practice questions
- Friday: Research reading or writing
- Saturday: Project build block
- Sunday: Weekly review, LinkedIn/portfolio update, and next-week plan

## Priority Rules

Priority 1:
Complete the major certifications and build portfolio projects tied to them.

Priority 2:
Publish portfolio evidence continuously, not only at the end.

Priority 3:
Use low-cost certificates to support the main story, not to create certificate overload.

Priority 4:
For every certificate, create a visible proof artifact: dashboard, SQL script, case study, memo, research brief, or LinkedIn post.
